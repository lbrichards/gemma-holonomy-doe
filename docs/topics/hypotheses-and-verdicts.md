## Narrative

Three hypotheses test different aspects of the holonomy signal. [H-mag](../glossary/h-mag.md) asks whether holonomy increases with [in-plane magnitude (pullback)](../glossary/in-plane-magnitude-pullback.md), pooled across plane types. [H-sem](../glossary/h-sem.md) — the powered, load-bearing claim — asks whether [real-feature plane](../glossary/real-feature-plane.md)s show materially greater holonomy than magnitude-matched [shuffled-feature plane](../glossary/shuffled-feature-plane.md)s after [covariate adjustment](../glossary/covariate-adjustment.md). [H-grad](../glossary/h-grad.md) asks whether holonomy rises monotonically: random < shuffled < real.

Each hypothesis receives a three-way verdict based on where its [confidence interval](../glossary/confidence-interval.md) sits relative to the [materiality threshold](../glossary/materiality-threshold.md) δ = log(1.25) ≈ 0.223 — a 25% multiplicative change on the original scale. [CORROBORATED verdict](../glossary/corroborated-verdict.md): CI lower bound above δ. [FALSIFIED verdict](../glossary/falsified-verdict.md): CI upper bound below δ. [INCONCLUSIVE verdict](../glossary/inconclusive-verdict.md): CI spans δ.

The [log-scale paired contrast](../glossary/log-scale-paired-contrast.md) d_b = log H_real − log H_shuffled is the primary test statistic for [H-sem](../glossary/h-sem.md), computed within each [base point](../glossary/base-point.md) to remove base-point-level variation. The [NULL-ATTRIBUTED verdict](../glossary/null-attributed-verdict.md) is a special falsification branch: if an unadjusted effect vanishes under [covariate adjustment](../glossary/covariate-adjustment.md), the signal came from [manifold distance](../glossary/manifold-distance.md) or [φ (plane angle)](../glossary/plane-angle.md), not from meaning.
