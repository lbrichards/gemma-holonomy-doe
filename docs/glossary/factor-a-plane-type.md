# Factor A (plane type)

<span style="background-color: #4051b5; color: white; padding: 0.2em 0.6em; border-radius: 0.25em; font-size: 0.85em; font-weight: 600;">COINED</span>

The first factor in the factorial design, with three levels (random, shuffled-feature, real-feature) representing a semantic gradient from noise floor to intact structure.

## Why this term exists

Factor A captures the semantic dimension of the experiment — whether the plane directions have any meaningful relationship to the base point's learned representation.
