# in-plane magnitude (pullback)

**`coined`**

The G-norm of a vector's G-orthogonal projection onto a plane, computed as mag(h) = sqrt(a^T M a) where M is the plane Gram matrix under the pullback metric.

## Why this term exists

Factor B (magnitude level) must be cut using a geometrically meaningful scalar. The pullback-metric in-plane magnitude respects the model's learned geometry rather than imposing Euclidean assumptions.
