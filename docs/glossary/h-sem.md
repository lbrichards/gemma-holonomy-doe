# H-sem

<span style="background-color: #4051b5; color: white; padding: 0.2em 0.6em; border-radius: 0.25em; font-size: 0.85em; font-weight: 600;">COINED</span>

The powered semantic hypothesis predicting that real-feature planes show materially greater holonomy than magnitude-matched shuffled-feature planes after covariate adjustment.

## Why this term exists

H-sem is the load-bearing claim of the study: that semantic coherence (joint activation) contributes to holonomy beyond what magnitude and feature geometry alone would explain.
