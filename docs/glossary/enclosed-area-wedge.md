# enclosed area (wedge)

<span style="background-color: #4051b5; color: white; padding: 0.2em 0.6em; border-radius: 0.25em; font-size: 0.85em; font-weight: 600;">COINED</span>

The pullback-metric area of the loop, computed as A_enclosed = ρ² √(det M), which normalizes the rotation angle to yield holonomy.

$$
A_{\text{enclosed}} = \rho^2 \sqrt{\det M}
$$

## Why this term exists

Using pullback-metric area rather than Euclidean area (ρ² sin φ) ensures all geometric quantities are computed under a single consistent metric, following the one-metric principle.
