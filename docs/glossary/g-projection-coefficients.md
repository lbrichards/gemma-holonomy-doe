# G-projection coefficients

<span style="background-color: #4051b5; color: white; padding: 0.2em 0.6em; border-radius: 0.25em; font-size: 0.85em; font-weight: 600;">COINED</span>

The coefficients a = M^{-1}(JD)^T(Jh) expressing the G-orthogonal projection of h onto the plane, used for center placement.

$$
a = M^{-1}(JD)^\top(Jh)
$$

## Why this term exists

The projection coefficients determine the in-plane component of the base activation under the pullback metric, enabling magnitude-matched center construction that preserves out-of-plane context.
