# one-metric principle

<span style="background-color: #4051b5; color: white; padding: 0.2em 0.6em; border-radius: 0.25em; font-size: 0.85em; font-weight: 600;">COINED</span>

The design choice to compute all geometric quantities (magnitude, area, transport) under a single consistent pullback metric G = J^T J.

## Why this term exists

Mixing Euclidean and pullback-metric quantities would introduce inconsistent notions of "distance" and "area." A unified metric ensures geometric coherence throughout the measurement pipeline.
